Agenda

10:00am - 10:15pm - Participants arrive

    We'll have lunch ready as you arrive and we'll eat together before the workshop program starts at noon

10:15am - 10:45am - Welcome, Introductions, Workshop Logistics

    Including a brief overview of Civic Switchboard project, and brief introductions from workshop participants

10:45pm - 12:05pm - Mapping Ecosystem Activity

    Why we advocate for ecosystem mapping; hands-on creation of your own ecosystem map; a chance to look at what other teams have produced. If you have done ecosystem mapping before, great! ...we'll have some prompts that will make this a fresh experience.

12:05pm – 1:30pm - Lunch and social time.

1:30pm – 3:00pm – Building Partnership Activity

    A brief typology of civic data roles that include libraries; teams consider a project to conduct as a team; use ecosystem map to situate your work and to identify who should be involved; teams create a worksheet capturing next steps. Plus share out

3:00pm – 3:30pm - Social Break / snacks

3:30pm – 4:15pm - Birds of a Feather Sessions

    BoF sessions take place -- small groups discussions.

4:15pm – 5:00pm - Wrap-up

    All back together -- What are your takeaways? What will you do when you are back home? Also some logistical wrap-up and workshop evaluation.

Workshop Code of Conduct

All attendees, speakers, sponsors and volunteers at the Civic Switchboard workshop are required to agree with the following code of conduct. Organizers will enforce this code throughout the event. We expect cooperation from all participants to help ensure a safe environment for everybody.

Need Help?
Contact us during the workshop by email at tutte@carnegielibrary.org (Eleanor), rmg44@pitt.edu (Bob), or by phone: 314 718 4088 (Eleanor) or 412 874 3255 (Bob), or talk to any Civic Switchboard organizer.

Civic Switchboard is dedicated to providing a harassment-free workshop experience for everyone, regardless of gender, gender identity and expression, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, religion (or lack thereof), or technology choices. We do not tolerate harassment of workshop participants in any form. Sexual language and imagery is not appropriate for any workshop venue, including talks, workshops, parties, Twitter and other online media. Workshop participants violating these rules may be sanctioned or expelled from the conference without a refund at the discretion of the conference organizers.

Harassment includes offensive verbal comments related to gender, gender identity and expression, age, sexual orientation, disability, physical appearance, body size, race, ethnicity, religion, technology choices, sexual images in public spaces, deliberate intimidation, stalking, following, harassing photography or recording, sustained disruption of talks or other events, inappropriate physical contact, and unwelcome sexual attention.

Participants asked to stop any harassing behavior are expected to comply immediately.

If a participant engages in harassing behavior, the workshop organizers may take any action they deem appropriate, including warning the offender or expulsion from the workshop with no refund.

If you are being harassed, notice that someone else is being harassed, or have any other concerns, please contact a member of the Civic Switchboard organizing team immediately.

Civic Switchboard organizers will be happy to help participants contact hotel/venue security or local law enforcement, provide escorts, or otherwise assist those experiencing harassment to feel safe for the duration of the conference. We value your attendance.

We expect participants to follow these rules at throughout the workshop and workshop-related social events.

In addition to having a code of conduct as an anti-harassment policy, in order to foster a productive and inclusive meeting environment, we ask that all workshop participants adhere to the following guidelines and principles for National Neighborhood Indicators Partnership meetings described here: https://www.neighborhoodindicators.org/library/guides/code-conduct-nnip-partner-meetings
